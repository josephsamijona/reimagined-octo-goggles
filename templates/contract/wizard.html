1. Independent Contractor Relationship

Relationship: Interpreter is engaged as an independent contractor and not as an employee of the Company. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employer-employee relationship, partnership, or joint venture.

Responsibility: Interpreter is responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes, insurance, licenses, and expenses related to services provided.

2. Scope of Services

Interpreter agrees to provide professional interpretation and/or translation services as requested, including on-site and remote assignments for courts, hospitals, and legal offices.

3. Compensation

Interpreter shall be compensated according to rates agreed upon per assignment. Compensation is issued only for completed assignments and approved no-shows.

4. Scheduling, Cancellations, and No-Shows

Interpreters are required to arrive on-site at least fifteen (15) minutes prior to the scheduled start time.

If a no-show occurs, it must be reported no earlier than 15 minutes after the start time. Failure to report correctly results in no compensation owed.

5. Attendance & Reliability

Interpreters are expected to maintain professional reliability. Performance issues include:

  • Late cancellations: Made less than 48 hours prior.
  • Tardiness: Failure to arrive 15 minutes early.

Corrective actions may include verbal/written warnings, reduced assignment priority, or removal from the active roster.

6. Confidentiality

Interpreter agrees to maintain strict confidentiality regarding all client information, medical information (HIPAA), and legal matters. This obligation survives termination of this Agreement.

7. Term and Termination

Agreement continues until terminated by either party with 30 days' written notice. Immediate termination may occur for breach of contract or misconduct.

8. Compliance with Laws

Interpreter agrees to comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws, and professional interpretation standards.

9. Equipment and Expenses

Interpreter is responsible for providing their own transportation, equipment, internet access, and tools required to perform services.

10. Assignment Acceptance

Acceptance of assignments is voluntary; however, repeated declines may affect future availability.

11. Non-Solicitation of Clients

During the term and for 24 months following, Interpreter agrees not to solicit or contract directly with any client introduced through the Company.

12. Dispute Resolution

Disputes shall first be addressed through good-faith mediation, then binding arbitration in the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.

13. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.

Acknowledgment & Agreement

14. Entire Agreement: This constitutes the entire agreement and supersedes all prior understandings.

15. Severability: If any provision is invalid, others remain in full force.
